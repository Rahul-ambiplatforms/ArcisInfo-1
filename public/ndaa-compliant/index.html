What is NDAA Section 889 and Why Does It Matter?

The National Defense Authorization Act (NDAA) Section 889 prohibits US federal agencies — and their contractors, subcontractors, and grant recipients — from procuring, using, or maintaining telecommunications and video surveillance equipment from five specific Chinese companies deemed national security risks.

This is not optional. Non-compliance can result in loss of federal contracts, debarment from government procurement, and significant legal liability.

  • 2019 Section 889(a)(1)(A): Federal agencies banned from buying covered equipment
  • 2020 Section 889(a)(1)(B): Agencies banned from contracting with orgs that USE covered equipment
  • 2022 FCC bans new authorizations for Hikvision/Dahua equipment in the US
  • 2024 Enforcement expands to include sub-contractors and grant recipients

Who Must Comply?

  • 🏛 Federal, state, and local government agencies
  • 🏗 Government contractors and sub-contractors
  • 🏦 Financial institutions (FFIEC regulated)
  • 🏫 Educational institutions receiving federal funding
  • 🏥 Healthcare facilities (HIPAA / HHS funded)
  • 🛢 Critical infrastructure operators (CISA)
  • 🌍 Companies selling to US government or military
  • 💼 GSA Schedule holders and contractors
